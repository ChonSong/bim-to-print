# Pilot roadmap — 18-month plan for a Sydney 3D-printed house

Phased execution plan taking a 3D concrete printing pilot from initial research to occupied dwelling.

---

## Phase 0 — Research & validation (Months 0–4)

| Month | Activity | Deliverables |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| 0–1 | Shortlist printer vendors | Decision matrix (done ✓) |
| 0–1 | Contact Contour3D, CyBe, Luyten | Vendor response; demo availability |
| 1–2 | Collect concrete mix proposals from 2–3 suppliers | Mix data sheets |
| 2–4 | Send sample prints to ULTRA Labs for structural testing | 28-day compressive/bond test report |
| 2–4 | Engage certifier (via Warne Properties or direct) | Signed engagement letter |
| 3–4 | Run material tests in `bim2print` with real mix parameters | Calibrated print settings |

**Budget: $8,000–21,000** (lab testing) + **$2,000–5,000** (certifier pre-engagement)

---

## Phase 1 — Design & compliance (Months 3–8)

| Month | Activity | Deliverables |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| 3–5 | Design pilot dwelling (2-bedroom, ~70 m²) | BIM model (IFC) |
| 4–6 | Submit preliminary DA to council | DA lodgement |
| 4–6 | Compile EoS dossier (see [evidence-of-suitability-dossier.md](evidence-of-suitability-dossier.md)) | Draft dossier for certifier review |
| 5–7 | Run fire-resistance test (FRL) at ULTRA Labs | Fire test report |
| 5–7 | Structural engineer review of design vs H1P1 | Signed certificate |
| 6–8 | Certifier review of EoS dossier | Comments → revisions |
| 7–8 | Obtain Construction Certificate (CC) | CC granted |

**Budget: $30,000–50,000** (design+engineering) + **$10,000–25,000** (lab testing supplement)

---

## Phase 2 — Procurement & site prep (Months 6–10)

| Month | Activity | Deliverables |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| 6–7 | Finalise printer partnership/purchase | Contract signed |
| 7–8 | Site selection and lease/approval | Site secured |
| 8–9 | Concrete mix qualification and batching | Batch tested and approved |
| 8–10 | Site preparation (level pad, access, utilities) | Site ready |
| 9–10 | Printer delivery and commissioning | Printer calibrated, test print |

**Budget: $80,000–200,000** (printer-dependent) + **$20,000–40,000** (site prep)

---

## Phase 3 — Print & monitor (Months 10–14)

| Month | Activity | Deliverables |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| 10–11 | Generate G-code from BIM model via `bim2print` | Production G-code |
| 10–11 | Pre-print inspection by certifier | Inspection pass |
| 11–13 | Print walls (2–4 months depending on printer speed and curing) | Completed wall system |
| 11–13 | In-process quality inspection (layer adhesion, dimensional) | QA reports |
| 13–14 | Post-print verification (core sampling, structural check) | Verification reports |

**Budget: $100,000–250,000** (print execution, concrete, labour)

---

## Phase 4 — Fit-out & commission (Months 12–17)

| Month | Activity | Deliverables |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| 12–14 | Roof structure installation | Roof complete |
| 13–15 | MEP rough-in (plumbing, electrical, data) | Services in wall |
| 14–16 | Interior finish (render, painting, flooring) | Interior complete |
| 15–17 | Exterior finish (rendering, cladding) | Exterior complete |
| 16–17 | Final inspection + Occupation Certificate (OC) | OC granted |

**Budget: $150,000–250,000** (fit-out, roof, services)

---

## Phase 5 — Handover & review (Months 16–18)

| Month | Activity | Deliverables |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| 16–17 | Post-occupancy monitoring (thermal, acoustic, structural) | Monitoring report |
| 17–18 | Lessons-learned report | Internal document |
| 17–18 | Cost analysis vs traditional build | Comparison spreadsheet |
| 18 | Public case study / press release | Marketing material |

**Budget: $10,000–20,000** (monitoring, documentation)

| Phase | Range | Notes |

| Phase 2 — Procurement & prep | $100,000–240,000 | Printer + site |
| Phase 3 — Print & monitor | $100,000–250,000 | Concrete + print ops |
| Phase 4 — Fit-out & commission | $150,000–250,000 | Roof, MEP, finishes |

| **Total** | **$410,000–861,000** | 2-bedroom pilot |

---

## Key assumptions

- 70 m² 2-bedroom dwelling
- Single-storey; simple rectangular footprint
- Printer partnership with Contour3D (service model) — zero printer capex
- Standard concrete mix + additive for printability
- Council in growth corridor (Liverpool, Camden, or Blacktown)
- Contingency: 20% on all line items

---

## Risk log

| Risk | Phase | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Certifier rejects EoS | 1 | Medium | High | Engage early; use Warne Properties as reference |
| Lab testing reveals mix failure | 0 | Medium | High | Run preliminary small-scale tests before full suite |
| Concrete supply chain (printable mix) | 2 | Low-Med | Medium | Test 2–3 suppliers in parallel during Phase 0 |
| Weather delays on-site | 3 | Medium | Medium | Print windows coincide with dry season (Sep–Apr) |
| Budget overrun | All | High | Medium | 20% contingency; phased approach allows stop-go |
| Printer unavailable (Contour3D only) | 2 | Low-Med | High | Maintain Luyten/CyBe as backup options |
